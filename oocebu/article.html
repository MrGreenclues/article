The Pulse of the Underground.

A deep dive into Cebu's resurgent street art scene and the creative collectives reclaiming the concrete canvas.

Author Carlos Verde

The grey monoliths of Cebu's industrial past are finding a new purpose. Not as factories, but as canvases for a resurgent creative class that refuses to be ignored. Under the moniker of flOOw, a loose collective of artists is turning the city's periphery into an open-air museum.

Watch Visual Documentary

It began in the quiet corners of Mandaue—a few spray-painted murals on abandoned warehouses. This movement is rooted in the heritage of the island but executed with a global edge.

"The city is our gallery, and every citizen is an invited guest."
— Marcus Graff, Collective Founder

As we look toward the future, the underground scene documented by flOOw provides a roadmap for Cebu's contemporary identity.
