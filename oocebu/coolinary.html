The Heritage of Puso.

Unpacking the architectural marvel of Cebu's hanging rice and the artisans preserving the craft.

Author Elena Sapor

In the humid morning air of Carbon Market, a rhythmic dance of fingers begins. This is the weaving of the Puso—the iconic hanging rice of Cebu—a culinary architecture that has survived centuries of colonial shifts.

Watch The Weaving Process

The "diamond" weave is not just aesthetic; it's functional. The tightly bound coconut leaves create a natural pressure cooker that allows the rice to expand without breaking the shell, resulting in the perfect texture.

Gallery Image 1
Gallery Image 2
"Every fold is a memory, and every knot is a connection to our ancestors' kitchen."
— Lola Basyon, Master Weaver

Today, as fast food threatens traditional methods, the Puso remains a stubborn symbol of Cebuano resilience and craftsmanship.
